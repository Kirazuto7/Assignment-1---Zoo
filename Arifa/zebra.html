Zebra

The zebra is an African equine distinguished by its black-and-white striped coat. This animal is found in savannahs, grasslands, woodlands and shrublands of eastern and southern Africa. It feeds mostly on grasses, but will also eat buds, fruits and roots. Social behavior differs among zebra species, but mountain and plains zebra live in harems consisting of an adult stallion and several mares. Due to their hides, these animals are highly vulnerable to poaching.

Fun Fact!

It has been theorized that the zebra's stripes help ward off biting flies.
