Koala

The koala is a type of marsupial, which is an animal that gives birth to underdeveloped young. This animal makes its home in the eucalyptus forests of southeastern and eastern Australia. It consumes about 500 grams of eucalyptus leaves daily. However, due to the limited nutritional value of these leaves, koalas are largely sedentary. Koalas are asocial, and bonding is only common among mothers and dependent young. Due to deforestation and bushfires, koalas were listed as Endangered in 2022.

Fun Fact!

Koalas are most active at night and around dawn and dusk.
