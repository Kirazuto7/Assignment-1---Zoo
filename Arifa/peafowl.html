Indian Peafowl

The Indian peafowl (peacocks refer to male peafowls and peahens refer to the females) resides in moist and dry-deciduous forests in the Indian subcontinent, as well as the drier lowlands of Sri Lanka. This bird feeds on fruits, seeds, insects, and even small reptiles and mammals. Males, which have more colorful feathers than the females, employ their decorative plumage in courtship rituals. Not only do the females lack this distinctive tail, but they are also smaller in size.

Fun Fact!

The peafowl is the national bird of India, where it is protected by law.
