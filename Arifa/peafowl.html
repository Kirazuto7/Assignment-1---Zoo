Indian Peafowl

The Indian peafowl (peacocks refer to male peafowls and peahens refer to the females) resides in moist and dry-deciduous forests in the Indian subcontinent, as well as the drier lowlands of Sri Lanka. This bird feeds on fruits, seeds, insects, and even small reptiles and mammals.

Fun Fact!

The peafowl is the national bird of India, where it is protected by law.
